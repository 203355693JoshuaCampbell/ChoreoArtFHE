# ChoreoArtFHE

**ChoreoArtFHE** is an interactive choreography art system powered by **Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE)**.  
It merges the movement of dancers with encrypted audience interactions to generate evolving, real-time performances.  
Every step, gesture, and rhythm is co-created ‚Äî yet completely private.  

By combining cryptography and performing arts, ChoreoArtFHE explores a new form of digital embodiment where data, motion, and emotion intertwine without ever compromising privacy.

## Concept Overview

In contemporary performance art, interactivity has become essential.  
However, audience participation often requires collecting personal or biometric data ‚Äî leading to privacy concerns and reduced authenticity in engagement.

**ChoreoArtFHE** introduces a paradigm where artistic expression and privacy coexist.  
Using **FHE**, dancers‚Äô movement data (captured via motion sensors) and audience reactions (e.g., gestures, emotion inputs, encrypted feedback) are **encrypted in real time** and **processed without decryption**.  
The result: a choreography that evolves based on encrypted signals ‚Äî a **collective artistic creation without exposure of individual data**.

## Why FHE Is Essential

Traditional interactive art systems rely on clear, unencrypted data streams ‚Äî leaving participants‚Äô identity, gestures, and emotional cues vulnerable.  
This not only poses privacy risks but also limits participation from audiences wary of being surveilled.

With **Fully Homomorphic Encryption**, ChoreoArtFHE performs **computation directly on encrypted data**, meaning:

- The system **never sees** the raw movements or emotions of any participant.  
- Even the server executing the choreography logic **cannot reconstruct** who did what.  
- The artistic outcome is **computed in total privacy**, ensuring both security and creative authenticity.

This creates a **trustless aesthetic system**, where collaboration happens through encryption rather than exposure.

## System Architecture

### 1. Capture Layer
- **Motion Capture Devices:** Sensors or cameras capture body movements.  
- **Edge Encryption:** Raw data is transformed into encrypted motion vectors using FHE before transmission.  

### 2. Audience Interface Layer
- Audience devices (e.g., mobile, AR glasses, biosensors) encrypt reactions locally.  
- Encrypted feedback is streamed to the Choreo Engine.

### 3. FHE Computation Layer
- The encrypted inputs from dancers and audience members are fused.  
- The system performs encrypted blending operations ‚Äî e.g., combining vectors, computing averages, generating transitions ‚Äî all without decryption.  
- The resulting encrypted performance model dictates lighting, rhythm, and movement adjustments.

### 4. Visualization Layer
- The decrypted result (only for visualization) is sent to stage controllers.  
- Lighting, sound, and projection mapping reflect the encrypted collaboration.

## Security & Privacy Model

- **End-to-End Encryption:** All data, from sensors to outputs, remains encrypted throughout.  
- **Key Isolation:** Each performer and participant maintains independent encryption keys.  
- **Non-Reversible Signals:** Motion patterns cannot be reverse-engineered to identify individuals.  
- **Computation Transparency:** The system logs cryptographic operations for verification without revealing data contents.  
- **No Central Authority:** ChoreoArtFHE can operate in a distributed, trust-minimized network for multi-venue performances.

## Technical Components

- **FHE Engine:** Enables algebraic manipulation of encrypted data streams.

- **Visualizer:** Securely translates decrypted summaries into real-time stage output.
